What is the Global Compact Board Programme?

The Global Compact Board Programme is the first of its kind to align and support Boards of Directors to more effectively oversee and drive their company’s sustainability strategy, with a view to protect and support financial value creation. Through facilitating strategic discussions among the Board on pressing sustainability issues and how to evolve the role of the Board and corresponding governance tools, the Programme aims to help Boards to:

  • Align on the strategic imperative and materiality of corporate sustainability as critical for the company’s long-term viability.
  • Realize the integral role that Board members can and should play in overseeing, incentivizing and driving corporate sustainability, embedding it into Board duties, governance mechanisms and structures.
  • Demonstrate leadership on Board adoption and oversight of corporate sustainability with investors, employees, customers and other stakeholders.
+
Who should participate in the Board Programme?

The Global Compact Board Programme is designed specifically for the elected or appointed members of the organizational body whose duty is to oversee, but not manage, the activities of the company. In many countries this body is called the Board of Directors, but in other countries is sometimes called the Board of Governors or the Supervisory Board. Board duties vary greatly across countries and jurisdictions, but typically include oversight of business strategy, risk management and legal compliance, as well as the recruitment and compensation of the chief executive.

+
What is the format of the Board Programme?

To ensure maximum impact and adequate level of discussion, the Board Programme is to be delivered in-house and organized in two module sessions, each three to four hours long. A minimum of 50% of the time is devoted to facilitated discussions among Board members.

+
Why was the Global Compact Board Programme created?

Board adoption and oversight of corporate sustainability, while on the rise in some respects, represents an area where vast improvement and gains can be achieved. In an effort to accelerate the full integration of corporate sustainability into business strategy and operations, the Global Compact Board Programme was created.

By embedding sustainability into its core duties – notably setting strategic course, risk management and monitoring, executive recruitment, and remuneration – Directors are uniquely positioned to ensure the full mainstreaming of sustainability into business strategy and practices in a way that will support the long-term profitability and viability of the company. The more a Director knows about sustainability issues and their management implications, the more likely he or she is to prioritize them as important to the company.

In fact, as the material impact of non-financial issues is recognized, leaders around the world are beginning to acknowledge that the fiduciary duty of Boards of Directors includes a responsibility to effectively manage risks and opportunities related to corporate sustainability and to respond to the interests and concerns of all stakeholders.

Who was involved in the development of the Board Programme?

The Global Compact Board Progamme was developed by Global Compact LEAD and the Principles for Responsible Management Education (PRME) – in collaboration with BCG, DLA Piper as knowledge partners and supported by BSR.

Content of the Board Programme

+
What is the scope of the Board Programme?

The Board Programme aims to trigger and facilitate strategic and important discussions at the Board level rather than offer ready solutions.

Included in the value proposition:

  • Stimulating discussion among Board Members on your most pressing sustainability issues
  • A path to integrating sustainability into the core strategy of your company
  • An ambitious and empowered Board ready to take on sustainability challenges
  • Ideas on how to evolve the role of the Board and corresponding governance tools

Not included in the value proposition:

  • Detailed benchmarking study of KPIs
  • Sustainability strategy development
  • Company-specific recommendations on the focus areas and ways of engagement
  • Complete design of governance tools with an implementation plan
+
What are Board members expected to discuss during the Board Programme Modules?

Key Questions in Module 1

  • What is your current performance in sustainability?
  • What will be the future imperatives for sustainability?
  • How do you address your material issues?
  • How can your company further integrate sustainability into strategy and business model?

Key Questions in Module 2

  • Why is sustainability a Board-level concern?
  • What is the role of the Board in sustainability oversight?
  • What governance tools will facilitate that?
  • What are the appropriate organization & skills?
  • What should be your action plan to drive change

Customization of the Board Programme

+
What does the Board Programme preparation and customization phase entail?

The Board Programme will be tailored to match the specific challenges and opportunities facing the particular company and Board. The customization will include everything from the selection of the facilitator, the detailed design of each module and the choice of issue briefs, caselets and best practices to be applied.

In addition, the top one to two levels of management will be asked to complete a brief survey on sustainability priorities and achievements, and one-to-one interviews with of five to seven Board Members and Members of the Executive Committee will be requested.

+
How long does it take to customize the Programme to the needs of my company?

Each customization process will have a unique timeline depending on the Board’s schedule and company expectations for the preparation and degree of customization. The preparation and customization process is however estimated to be about two to three months, accounting for time needed to gather and develop resources and integrate feedback from the company undertaking the Programme.

+
Who is involved in the customization of the Board Programme?

Four functions are required for a successful Programme preparation, customization and delivery: company core team, facilitator, customization support and the Global Compact (coordinator).

The participating company is required to identify a company core team responsible for the customization input and for participating in regular preparatory meetings with the facilitator, customization team and the Global Compact (either in-person or through tele/video conferencing).

The recommended size of the company team is three to four representatives. As a minimum, the team should consist of a sustainability lead and a representative from the Secretariat to the Board of Directors. It is also recommended that the Programme delivery and preparation are actively sponsored by a C-suite executive to ensure efficient management alignment. The company is required to ensure that sufficient resources are allocated to support interviews, questionnaires or gathering other necessary data in a timely manner.

+
How are Board Programme facilitators selected?

A world-class facilitator is key to the successful delivery of the Global Compact Board Programme. Companies will have the opportunity to choose from a roster of professional facilitators from a variety of industries, with corporate governance and sustainability expertise.

Acknowledging the importance of industry-specific dynamics, facilitators are selected based on their ability to combine global and local contexts, as well as to understand the nuance of corporate governance in different jurisdictions. Over time, the roster will grow to include facilitators with fluency in all six United Nations languages (Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Russian and Spanish). Seasoned business executives, such as members of the Global Compact Board, will also be invited to support the delivery of the Programme on a case-by-case basis.

  • The Global Compact ensures that all candidates listed on the Facilitator Roster are knowledgeable about the Board Programme content and well qualified for Board-level discussion.
  • If a participating company has its own suggestions for facilitators not yet on the official roster, the Global Compact welcomes the opportunity to consider, but reserves the right to accept or decline the suggested candidates after an assessment of experience and expertise.

Participating in the Board Programme

+
Is my company eligible to participate in the Board Programme?

The Global Compact Board Programme is available to all Global Compact companies with relevant governance models. If you are not a Global Compact participant, but wish to undertake the Board Programme, please contact us at gcboardprogramme@unglobalcompact.org

+
How do we ensure that the information shared before and during the Programme is kept confidential?

If the company requires it, the facilitator and/or customization team will sign appropriate confidentiality agreements to access information needed for customization (the company’s own template is recommended to be used).

+
Is there a cost for the Board Programme?

The Board Programme is offered at commercial rate, covering the services provided by the Global Compact, the facilitator and the customization team involved in the Programme development for the participating company. Contact the Global Compact (gcboardprogramme@unglobalcompact.org) to learn more about the facilitators and selected customization partners available.

+
Can the Programme be delivered in other languages than English?

Language is a critical factor to the success of the Board Programme. In case the participating company sees a need for translators attending any of the interviews and/or simultaneous interpreters (SI) for the workshop delivery, it is the responsibility of the company to secure this service unless the selected facilitator and customization team can provide services in the relevant language. Guidance on the ideal set up and timeline can be provided by the Global Compact.
